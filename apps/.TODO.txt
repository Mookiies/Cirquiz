  enwiki-latest-pages-articles1.xml.bz2 (and 2, 3, etc.)
  The full English Wikipedia dump is split into numbered chunks. You could download just the first few. Each file is ~200-300MB and covers a alphabetical slice of articles. Not ideal since you'd get A-C
  heavily and miss later letters.

  DBpedia or Wikipedia article subsets
  There are pre-filtered HuggingFace datasets that are subsets of English Wikipedia, curated by topic or quality. For example:
  - wikimedia/wikipedia on HuggingFace — the full English Wikipedia as a streaming dataset, no download required, and you can filter by category/length before processing
  - Much easier than downloading a raw XML dump

  simplewiki + targeted enwiki categories
  Keep simplewiki as the base, then pull specific enwiki article dumps for categories where you want more depth (e.g. Science, History).

  The HuggingFace streaming approach is probably the best middle ground for your use case — you'd replace the dump download + mwxml parsing in generate.py with a load_dataset("wikimedia/wikipedia",
  "20231101.en", streaming=True) call, filter articles by length and keyword, and feed them into the same chunking/generation loop. No multi-GB download sitting on disk, and you can stop whenever you have
  enough questions.
